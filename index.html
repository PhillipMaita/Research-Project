Splits

Before presenting the different splits popularized in the world of bodybuilding, what is a split. Very simply a split is another word for schedule. Its usually split up by like 2 or 3 days and repeats with a rest day inbetween (Ex. Push, pull, legs repeats once with one rest day). Reminder these are all suggestion and this info is just everything I have learned there is way more splits than these, these are just the ones I have tried.

Push, Pull, Legs
Arnold
"Bro" Split
Push, Pull, Legs:

Push, Pull, Legs is probably one of if not the most popular split used in the gym. Its super simple and easy to remember, and is easily followed. I'm currently usuang this split and its great. Every muscle is hit twice a week and I have seen some signifigant gains from it.

    1. Push: Chest, Triceps, Shoulders (side delt / optional front delt) 2. Pull: Back, Bicpes, (optional rear delts) 3. Legs
Arnold:

Arnold spit is a little less known than push, pull, legs but still pretty good. As the name suggests its the split that Arnold used back in the day. It's split up by muscles not by movements like push, pull, legs. Every muscle is hit twice a week and it's effective, although I have not tried it myself.

    1. Chest and Back 2. Sarms (shoulders, biceps, triceps) 3. Legs
"Bro" split:

Bro split is even less known than most splits. It's a combination of both Arnold and push, pull, legs. It has upsides like the variability in exercises which makes it more intersting, although that variation can get confusing. A big downside is that some muscles dont get 2 days of rest and some get more which isn't exactly optimal. It's not a bad split, I myself used it when I started out and saw the majoirty of my early gains from it, theres just more optimal splits out there.

    1. Push: Chest, Triceps, Shoulders (side delt / optional front delt) 2. Pull: Back, Bi's, (optional rear delts) 3. Legs 4. Chest and Back 5. Sarms (shoulders, biceps, triceps) 6. Legs
